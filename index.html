Welcome

This is Toby Ziegler's online place. Enjoy your visit!

I have been calling this project:

Pride Goeth Before the Rebuild

I had built these pages with pride. I had built these pages with care. I did it all by myself.

Now I just can't stand them. They've got to go. You are looking at the first page of tbe new replacements.

I had carefully hand-crafted every line of code. Raw HTML, CSS, JavaScript and PHP. No dependencies. Everything from scratch. Often inspired by others, but always worked out and implemented by me, myself and I.

I spent so much time getting everything right, functionally, visually, you name it -- that I didn't ever get to add but a fraction of the actual content I wanted to show here.

So it has to go. I am bringing in Bootstrap, jQuery, Angular, and every other dependency I once eschewed. Well, the ones which will make things go faster and allow me to focus more on the content and style of these pages, instead of just the mechanics.

Getting mechanics right is fine, and I love doing so. Unfortunately, unless I point out those bits in code snippets, almost no one is going to be able to appreciate them but the afore-mentioned me, myself and I. While I don't need anyone else's approval, explicit or otherwise, one of the primary reasons I am putting this here is to share.

Enjoy looking around while I eat a tremendous slice of humble pie.

Parallax

I have been avoiding building in parallax. Mostly because I am not one to jump on the latest trend just because it is trendy. Just because everyone is doing it, doesn't make it right.

It doesn't make it wrong, either.

So, I found a pure css parallax example by Keith Clark to use as my starting point. Keith did a great job of laying out the basics and explaining the whys. Thanks, Keith.

So, maybe you like this parallax page, maybe you don't. Either way, it won't last forever, and I have enjoyed exploring the process of putting it all together.
